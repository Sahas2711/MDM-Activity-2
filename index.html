FEATURES

Input Features

min_price

float64

Minimum price recorded at the mandi. Acts as a floor indicator and correlates with demand troughs.

max_price

float64

Maximum price recorded. Represents peak demand and serves as a strong predictor for modal price trends.

Why these features? min_price and max_price have the highest Pearson correlation with modal_price (target). They capture the price band within which actual transactions occur.

TARGET

Binary Classification

0

Low Price

modal_price ≤ ₹3,600

⏳ HOLD — Wait for better price
1

High Price

modal_price > ₹3,600

✓ SELL — Favorable market

Threshold Logic: ₹3,600 chosen as the median modal price across the dataset, creating a balanced binary split for classification models.

OUTLIER DETECTION

DBSCAN Insights

Why DBSCAN?

Unlike K-Means, DBSCAN doesn't require specifying K. It finds natural clusters AND labels outliers as noise — crucial for detecting price spikes and market anomalies.

Outlier Detection

Points not belonging to any dense region are classified as noise — these represent unusual price events (droughts, policy changes).

0.001

32

Samples per gradient update. Balances memory usage and training stability.

LIMITATIONS

Current Limitations

Data Dependency

Model accuracy heavily depends on AGMARKNET data quality. Missing records, delayed updates, or reporting errors directly impact predictions.

Regional Bias

21 states covered, but data density varies significantly. States with fewer records may produce less reliable predictions.

No Real-Time Data

Current system uses historical data only. Lacks integration with live price feeds, weather APIs, or real-time news streams.
